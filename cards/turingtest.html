The Turing Test and First Steps

1950s

Brilliant mathematician Alan Turing historically asked the primary question: "Can machines think?". He presented an experimental benchmark setup known as the imitation game, or the Turing Test, evaluating a machine's capacity to exhibit intelligent behavior indistinguishable from humans.

During the 1950s, initial programs capable of playing chess and sorting basic logic problems surfaced, officially giving birth to the term "Artificial Intelligence".
