The Era of Personal Computers

1970s - 1990s

The creation of microprocessors led to the sudden emergence of personal computers (PCs). Leading technology corporations like Apple and IBM made highly sophisticated systems accessible to ordinary households and office workers.

During this iconic timeframe, graphical user interfaces (GUI) replaced textual terminal strings, and the global Internet network was born, entirely transforming human knowledge exchange.
