Transistors and Integrated Circuits

1950s - 1960s

The invention of the transistor in 1947 was a true revolution. Computers became smaller, more reliable, and faster than ever before. The advent of integrated circuits (microchips) paved the way for mass production of affordable electronics.

Microchips compressed millions of components onto a single silicon wafer, completely changing our capability to process complex data and build powerful consumer hardware systems.
