Evolution of Technology

From the first mechanical calculators to artificial intelligence and quantum computers. Discover how technology has changed our world and what awaits us in the future.

The Dawn and Development of Computers

The Era of Vacuum Tubes and Punch Cards (ENIAC)

1940s

The first electronic computers, such as ENIAC, occupied entire rooms, weighed tens of tons, and required immense amounts of energy. They were programmed using switches and punch cards.

Transistors and Integrated Circuits

1950s - 1960s

The invention of the transistor was a true revolution. Computers became smaller, more reliable, and faster. The advent of integrated circuits (microchips) paved the way for mass production of computers.

The Era of Personal Computers

1970s - 1990s

The creation of microprocessors led to the emergence of PCs. Companies like Apple and IBM made computers accessible to ordinary people. Graphical user interfaces and the Internet were born.

The Mobile Revolution

2000s - Present

The computing power of past supercomputers now fits in your pocket. Smartphones and tablets have changed how we communicate, work, and consume information.

The Evolution of Artificial Intelligence

The Turing Test and First Steps

1950s

Alan Turing asked, "Can machines think?" The first programs capable of playing chess and solving logical puzzles appeared. The term "artificial intelligence" itself was born.

Expert Systems and the "AI Winter"

1970s - 1980s

Systems mimicking the decision-making of human experts were created. However, due to a lack of computing power and inflated expectations, a period of reduced funding and interest began — the "AI Winter."

Machine Learning and Neural Networks

2010s

Thanks to massive amounts of data (Big Data) and powerful graphics cards (GPUs), neural networks made a breakthrough. AI learned to recognize images, understand speech, and defeat world champions in the game of Go.

Generative AI

2020s

The emergence of Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT. AI is now capable of writing code, composing poems, drawing photorealistic pictures, and conducting meaningful dialogue at a human level.

The Future of Technology

Quantum Computing

Future

Quantum computers, using qubits, will be able to solve problems in seconds that would take modern supercomputers thousands of years. This will revolutionize cryptography, medicine, and materials science.

Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI)

Future

Connecting the brain directly to a computer will not only allow the treatment of neurological diseases but also enable controlling devices with the power of thought and exchanging information on a fundamentally new level.

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

Future

The creation of human-level artificial intelligence (AGI) — a machine capable of self-awareness and solving any intellectual task accessible to a human. This will become the point of technological singularity.

The Internet of Senses

Future

The development of VR and AR will lead to the creation of a digital reality where we can transmit not only sound and images but also smells, tastes, and tactile sensations over the network.
